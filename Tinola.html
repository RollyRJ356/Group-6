    Description

    Tinola is a Filipino soup usually served as an appetizer or a main entrée with white rice. Traditionally, this dish is cooked with chicken or fish, wedges of chayote, and leaves of the siling labuyo chili pepper in broth flavored with ginger, onions and fish sauce.

  • Ingredients:
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 Onion, chooped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (1½ inch) piece fresh ginger, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 3 pounds chicken legs and thighs, rinsed and patted dry
  • 2 (14 ounce) cans chicken broth
  • 1 chayote squash, peeled and cut into bite-sized pieces
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 head bok choy, chopped
  • ½ pound spinach
  • How to cook:
  • Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat; cook and stir the onion and garlic in the hot oil until fragrant.
  • Quickly stir the ginger and fish sauce into the onion and garlic mixture before adding the chicken; cook together for 5 minutes.
  • Add the chayote to the mixture and simmer until the chicken is no longer pink in the center, about 10 minutes more.
  • Pour the chicken broth over the mixture and cook another 5 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  • Add the bok choy and spinach; cook until the spinach is just wilted, 1 to 2 minutes.
  • Serve hot.
